Welcome Session - Action Games
Activity 1: Superhero Training Camp
Activity Goal -
To engage children in imaginative and physical play while encouraging them to develop teamwork, creativity, and coordination skills.
Materials Needed -
  • Pillows, hula hoops, tunnels, and other simple obstacle course items
  • Upbeat music
  • Construction paper, markers, stickers, and other craft supplies
  • Inflated balloons
  • Stuffed animals
Activity Guidelines
Obstacle Course -
  1. Set up the obstacle course in a safe area, either indoors or outdoors.
  2. Guide the children through the course, encouraging them to use their superhero powers to complete the challenges.
  3. Make sure each child gets a turn to navigate the course.
Freeze Dance -
  1. Play lively music and have the kids dance freely.
  2. Pause the music at intervals and instruct the children to freeze in superhero poses when the music stops.
  3. Restart the music and continue the game.
Superhero Craft -
  1. Provide the children with craft supplies and instruct them to create their superhero masks and capes.
  2. Encourage them to use their creativity to design their unique superhero attire.
Balloon Sword Fights -
  1. Give each child an inflated balloon and teach them how to have safe, playful sword fights.
  2. Emphasize the importance of not hurting others during the game.
Save the Stuffed Animals -
  1. Scatter stuffed animals around the room.
  2. Designate one child as the superhero and instruct them to "save" the animals without touching the floor (the "lava").
  3. Rotate roles so that each child gets a chance to be the superhero.
Before the Week -
  • Prepare the obstacle course items and ensure a safe play area.
  • Gather craft supplies for the superhero mask and cape activity.
  • Inflate enough balloons for the balloon sword fights.
Play Session - Silly Songs
Activity Manual: Silly Monkey Dance
Activity Goal -
The goal of this activity is to engage preschool-aged children (up to 6 years old) in a fun and active playtime session, promoting physical activity, creativity, and social interaction.
Materials Needed -
  1. Audio playback device (e.g., a smartphone or a portable speaker)
  2. A device with access to the "Silly Monkey Dance" song (e.g., a playlist or YouTube)
  3. A clear, open space for children to move around
  4. Optional: Monkey-themed props (e.g., plush toys, banana cutouts)
Preparation (To Be Done One Week Prior) -
  1. Select a suitable location with enough space for kids to move and dance comfortably.
  2. Ensure you have access to a device that can play the "Silly Monkey Dance" song.
  3. Familiarize yourself with the song and its lyrics.
  4. Optional: Prepare any monkey-themed props or decorations to create a playful atmosphere.
Activity Guidelines
Introduction (5 minutes) -
  1. Gather the children in the designated play area.
  2. Briefly explain the activity and its purpose: "Today, we're going to have a blast doing the 'Silly Monkey Dance.' We'll dance and have fun like silly monkeys!"
  3. Optionally, show the children any monkey-themed props to generate excitement.
Activity (15 minutes) -
  1. Play the "Silly Monkey Dance" song using the audio playback device.
  2. Encourage the children to listen to the song and follow the instructions for different monkey movements (e.g., stomping, clapping, swinging, jumping).
  3. Join in with the children and demonstrate the movements as the song plays.
  4. Create an enthusiastic and playful atmosphere, cheering the kids on as they dance and have fun.
  5. Let the children's creativity shine; they can add their own silly monkey moves as well.
Cool Down (5 minutes) -
  1. Gradually slow down the music and guide the children in taking deep breaths to cool down.
  2. Have the children sit or gather in a circle.
  3. Share positive feedback, applaud their efforts, and ask if they had fun dancing like silly monkeys.
Instructions for Trainers -
  1. Maintain a positive and encouraging tone throughout the activity.
  2. Be prepared to join in with the children to create a fun and engaging atmosphere.
  3. Pay attention to each child, and ensure they are safe during the activity.
  4. Encourage children to be creative and expressive with their dance moves.
  5. Be patient, as some children may need a bit more time to warm up and participate fully.
Conclusion -
The "Silly Monkey Dance" activity is a fantastic way to engage young children in a fun and active playtime session. By following this manual and creating an exciting and inclusive atmosphere, teachers can help kids explore their creativity, enjoy physical activity, and develop social interaction skills. Remember to have a wonderful time dancing like silly monkeys together!
